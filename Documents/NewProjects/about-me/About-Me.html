Introduction

I build websites. As a former chemistry teacher, I'm still passionate about making science more accessible to the public. Science concepts can be explained (and more appreciated) effectively by talking about them in the context of the current world we live in. For exammple, why is it a big deal that the temperature of Earth's oceans has risen by about 1 ℃? It's only 1 ℃, right?

The key is to show the relevance of chemistry, biology, and physics to our everyday lives. They can't be relegated to a textbook, where they're associated with dry lectures for high school and college students. Whether you're 8 or 80, you can challenge the status quo in any field -- thinking "outside the box" is the only way to move forward in life. You have the power to positively contribute to our planet. Technology might be the most efficient tool for this.

How? What if there was an app that shows you the content of your drinking water (Flint, anyone?), and that app connected you to your local House representative to report it?

I also debate on a daily basis whether I should give up coffee. There are pros on both sides!
